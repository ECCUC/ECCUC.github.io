About the Psychometric Chart

Originally developed by the Israeli architect Baruch Givoni, this chart serves as a tool to identify the comfort zones in Chilean living spaces.

The psychometric chart visually represents the intricate interplay between temperature, absolute humidity, and the enthalpy of the air.

This specific psychometric chart is crafted using academic data from the Civil Construction school of the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile (PUC).

The psychometric chart is a powerful tool for understanding the environmental conditions that contribute to human comfort. It plots key parameters like temperature (X Axis) and absolute humidity (Y axis), providing a graphical representation of the confort inside living places

In practical terms, the chart helps professionals in the fields of architecture and HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning) design to assess and optimize indoor environmental conditions. By identifying the comfort zone on the chart, one can determine whether a given combination of temperature and humidity levels is conducive to human well-being.

Understanding the psychometric chart is crucial for creating spaces that are not only aesthetically pleasing but also ensure optimal comfort and well-being for occupants.


About the sensors

These sensors are strategically located in Santiago, Chile, capturing comprehensive temperature and absolute humidity data over the course of an entire year.

About the data

The data for these sensors originates from PurpleAir, a leading provider of air quality monitoring solutions. PurpleAir's network of sensors provides accurate and real-time information on air quality, contributing to our understanding of environmental conditions in Santiago, Chile.

How the Psychometric Chart Works

By selecting a sensor from the dropdown menu (or selecting the sensor on the map), you can visualize the data from that specific location on the psychometric chart. The chart will display the temperature and absolute humidity data for the selected sensor, allowing you to identify the comfort zone for that specific location.
